# NCE-001 Route Development Report — Phase A Summary

**Report ID:** INT-2025-018
**Author:** alice.chemist@example.com
**Date:** 2025-11-12
**Project:** NCE-001

## Executive summary

The current working route to the NCE-001 API proceeds in four steps from
commercially available starting materials. The key Suzuki-Miyaura coupling
(Step 1) has been optimised to 73% isolated yield on 100 mg scale; scale-up
to 10 g is pending. Protodehalogenation is the dominant side-product on
runs with older Pd(PPh₃)₄ lots.

## Step 1 — Suzuki-Miyaura coupling

- Aryl bromide + phenylboronic acid → 4-cyanobiphenyl
- Conditions: Pd(PPh₃)₄ 5 mol%, K₂CO₃ 2 equiv, toluene / EtOH / H₂O, 85 °C, 16 h.
- Best result: 73% yield (ELN-NCE001-0042).
- Pd(dppf)Cl₂ gave 58% (ELN-NCE001-0043) — dppf underperforms for this substrate.

### Observations

- HPLC purity 98.4% after silica column.
- No significant homocoupling by LC-MS.
- Column loading > 80 mg / g silica gave tailing; keep loading ≤ 50 mg / g.

## Step 2 — nitrile reduction (deferred)

DIBAL-H and LiAlH₄ both under evaluation; preliminary results indicate
LiAlH₄ at –78 °C gives cleaner conversion, but scale-up safety review pending.

## Risks / open items

- Pd contamination on API: need to quantify residual Pd after Step 1 by
  ICP-MS. Spec per ICH Q3D is ≤ 10 μg/day.
- Aqueous waste stream contains trace Pd; engineering to confirm disposal
  compliance.
